Includes rights arising from human innovation and creativity, including but not limited to patents, trademarks, copyrights, industrial designs, trade secrets, designs, databases, plant varieties, and any protectable subject matter under applicable Iraqi laws.

Intellectual Property Management
Measures taken by Dallali to protect, organize, and document its intellectual property rights or those of contributing parties in accordance with Iraqi laws.
Patent
An official certificate issued under the Iraqi Patents and Industrial Designs Law No. 65 of 1970 (as amended), granting an exclusive right to exploit an invention for a specified period, subject to novelty, originality, and industrial applicability.
Work (Protected Work)
Any literary, artistic, or scientific work—written, spoken, visual, or audio—protected under Iraqi Copyright Law No. 3 of 1971 (as amended).
Author
The person who created the Work and holds moral and financial rights under Iraqi law.
Copyright
Includes financial and moral rights such as publishing, translation, and commercial exploitation, as well as attribution and protection against prejudicial modification.

Trademark
Any sign used or intended to distinguish goods or services of one entity from those of others under Iraqi Trademarks Law No. 21 of 1957 (as amended). It may include words, names, symbols, letters, numbers, colors, shapes, scents, sounds, or any distinctive element.
Industrial Designs
Any two-dimensional design or three-dimensional shape that gives a product a distinctive appearance (not purely functional), under Iraqi law and related instructions.
Trade Secrets
Information of commercial value that is not widely known, for which reasonable steps are taken to maintain confidentiality.
Exclusive License
An agreement granting a licensee exclusive rights to exploit IP and preventing the licensor from granting the same rights to others during the license term.
Non-Exclusive License
An agreement allowing exploitation of IP while the licensor may grant similar licenses to other parties under agreed terms.
Dallali
The entity responsible for operating Dallali services and systems in Iraq, including its management, staff, contractors, and legal representatives.

4) Real Estate Advertising Standards
First: Scope of Application
These standards apply to all real estate advertisements published through Dallali within the Republic of Iraq, including properties, lands, and real estate auctions.
Second: General Advertiser Obligations
Accuracy and Integrity: Ensure correctness of all published information.
No Misleading Content: Do not use statements/images that may mislead regarding property, price, or terms.
Compliance with Laws: Comply with Iraqi laws and relevant competent authorities’ instructions.
Ethical Content: Do not publish content that violates public morals or attacks beliefs, individuals, or institutions.
Official Naming: Use official names of cities, governorates, districts, neighborhoods, and property types; commonly used names may be added for clarity.
Third: Mandatory Information in Ads
Each advertisement must clearly include:
Clear indication that the content is an advertisement.
Property type (residential, commercial, industrial, agricultural, etc.).
Purpose (sale, rent, investment, auction).
Advertiser name and capacity (owner, authorized representative, office, licensed broker, etc.).
Professional license number (if applicable).
Location (governorate, district/sub-district, neighborhood/area, plot number if available).
Active contact method (phone, email, or office address).
Area/size (sqm or appropriate unit).
Legal status (any dispute, mortgage, or restriction).
Utilities/services (electricity, water, sewage, etc.).
Estimated age (if built) and any factors affecting decisions (e.g., warranties).
Fourth: Ads on Dallali
In addition to the above, ads must include:
Detailed description (layout, façade, street width, and key value factors).
Boundaries/lengths for lands or large plots (as much as possible).
Price/consideration (sale price, rent, investment value, auction price).
Fifth: Real Estate Auction Ads
In addition to the above, auction ads must include:
Auction license number (if required by Iraqi law).
Location, specific time, and duration of the auction.
General auction terms (participation requirements, payment methods, guarantees).
Submission deadline (if applicable).
Sixth: Prohibited Content
Ads must not include:
Violations of public morals or societal values in Iraq.
Negative attacks on competitors, offices, or individuals.
Discriminatory statements based on race, religion, sect, or affiliation.
False/misleading information or images unrelated to the property.
Government names/logos or state symbols without authorization.
Third-party IP infringement (unlicensed photos, plans, or materials).

Eighth: Removal of Expired Ads

Escalation: Serious or repeated violations may be referred to competent Iraqi authorities for legal action.
